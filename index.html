⚡ How to Use This App

  • Attempt before reveal: Say your answer aloud before tapping Show Answer. Effortful retrieval is the whole point — passive re-reading doesn't build memory.
  • Rate honestly: Tap Again/Hard/Good/Easy. Only tap Easy if you recalled it instantly and completely. Self-deception wastes revision time.
  • 10 cards = 15 min max: Set a timer. Urgency sharpens focus. Don't binge. Spaced repetition beats marathon sessions every time.
  • Pearl → Patient: When you read the clinical pearl, visualise a real patient from your wards. Emotional context anchors memory far better than rote repetition.
  • "Again" cards first: Before starting new cards, spend 5 min revisiting yesterday's flagged cards. This is the spacing effect — your brain consolidates during sleep.

🎯 Exam Strategy

  • Triads first: Examiners love asking for exact components. Nail Beck's, Virchow's, Charcot's (×2!), Whipple's, Reynold's Pentad as a priority.
  • Know the traps: Two Charcot's triads (biliary vs neurological). Two Trousseau's signs (malignancy vs hypocalcaemia). These are guaranteed NEET PG questions.
  • Mechanisms over memorisation: If you understand WHY Beck's triad happens, you'll never forget it — and you'll handle modified questions too.
